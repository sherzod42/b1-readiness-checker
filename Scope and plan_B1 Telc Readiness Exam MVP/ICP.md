# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
## B1 German Exam Preparation — Solo Founder Product

**Document Purpose:** This ICP serves as the foundational reference for all marketing activities — content strategy, YouTube videos, blog posts, ad copy, landing pages, email sequences, and social media. It captures who our ideal customer is, what they're struggling with, what they truly want, and how our product uniquely solves their problem. This document also provides context for AI assistants working on this project.

**Last Updated:** March 2026

---

## Executive Summary

This product targets **immigrants and expats in the DACH region** who must pass the **telc Deutsch B1 or Goethe-Zertifikat B1** exam for residency, citizenship, or career purposes. The hero customer is under time pressure, has likely tried multiple solutions that failed them, and needs a structured, affordable, self-paced preparation path that covers the core exam sections: **Lesen (Reading), Hören (Listening), and Sprachbausteine (Grammar/Vocabulary)**.

**Business Model:** Solo founder selling a structured 30-day digital course (hosted on Teachable) + interactive exercise pages, with a YouTube channel as the primary long-term acquisition engine.

**Core Differentiation:**
- Structured, exam-specific 30-day course (not generic language learning)
- Each day: video lesson + AI speaking prompt + vocab list + interactive exercise
- Interactive exercises for Lesen, Hören, and Sprachbausteine — playable directly in the browser, no signup needed
- Speaking practice via AI (Claude/ChatGPT voice mode) — affordable alternative to tutors
- Price point under €50 (vs. €1,200+ for traditional courses, €200+ for exam retakes)
- YouTube channel builds trust and authority before purchase

**Exam Scope:** telc and Goethe exams. Starting with B1 as the largest segment.

## Current Solutions & Why They Fail

### 1. Language Schools (VHS, Goethe Institut, Private Schools)
**Why they fail:** Extremely expensive (€1,200+), fixed schedules, not always exam-focused, limited individual attention.

### 2. Apps (Duolingo, Babbel, Busuu)
**Why they fail:** Not exam-specific, gamification creates false sense of progress, no accountability or structured exam prep.

### 3. Private Tutors
**Why they fail:** Expensive (€30–50/hour × 20+ hours), inconsistent quality, scheduling challenges.

### 4. Self-Study (Books, YouTube, Free Resources)
**Why they fail:** No structure or accountability, overwhelming amount of content, no feedback loop, no exam simulation.

### 5. ChatGPT / General AI Tools
**Why they fail:** No exam-specific structure, no curriculum or progression, user has to know what to ask.

## Objections & Responses

### "I've tried apps before, they don't work"
**Response:** This is not a gamified app — it's a structured 4-week exam preparation course. No points, no streaks. Just a daily plan that covers exactly what the telc B1 tests.

### "Is this enough for the real exam?"
**Response:** Every exercise is built around real telc B1 exam formats. Reading passages, listening exercises, and Sprachbausteine all mirror what you'll face on exam day.

### "I can do it myself with YouTube and ChatGPT"
**Response:** You could — but will you? Most people who say this still don't have a preparation strategy weeks before their exam. This course removes the guesswork and gives you a structured path.

### "I don't have time"
**Response:** The course is designed for busy professionals. Each day takes 20–30 minutes. You can fit it around work, family, and life.
